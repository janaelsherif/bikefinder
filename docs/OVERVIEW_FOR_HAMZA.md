# EuropeBikeFinder — overview for Hamza

What each part of the app is for, and **which websites** are used to pull or compare information.

---

## What the app is for

**PatrickBike** uses this tool to see **European e-bike offers** in one workspace, with amounts shown in **Swiss francs (CHF)** and a rough view of import and delivery—so you can compare with the **Swiss market** and plan buying or sourcing. It is **not** a public shop.

Figures are **estimates**. Before you buy, always check **current customs, VAT, and the seller’s terms**.

You can use the app in **English** or **German (Switzerland)**. If your team turns on **sign-in**, you’ll enter the password they give you first.

## Competitive pricing — in plain language

This page helps when you already have a **specific bike in mind** (one you bought or are about to price). You enter **brand**, **model**, **condition grade**, and your **total buy-in in CHF** (purchase plus refurb, parts—whatever you consider your landed cost).

The tool then:

- Looks for **similar bikes already sitting in the workspace** (same kind of listings as on the home page) and works out a **typical price band in CHF** (middle of the market, plus a “lower” and “higher” slice so you see spread—not just one number).
- **When your team has turned on the extra option**, it can also **look at agreed Swiss competitor shop pages at that moment** and pull **current list prices** shown there for the same search. If that is off or not enough data, it **falls back** to the stored similar listings so you still get an answer.
- Shows a **suggested list price in CHF** that respects a **minimum price** so you do not accidentally price **below your margin** on your buy-in.

You also get a **short table of example listings** (with links) so you can sanity-check numbers against real ads. None of this replaces your own judgment, customs, or the seller’s terms—it is a **decision support** screen, not an official appraisal.

**Note:** The **“% below Swiss market”** on listing cards elsewhere in the app uses a **different** Swiss reference (a fixed benchmark table by brand/category). The pricing page is tuned for **this bike + your buy-in**, so the two percentages are **not meant to match line-for-line**.

## Competitor watch (market intelligence) — in plain language

Here “market intelligence” means **repeatable, comparable snapshots** of the **public storefronts** you care about—not a full business report.

For each agreed competitor, the app keeps **counts** such as **how many e-bike offers** show up on the category or search you monitor, and **how that count changed** since the last snapshot (roughly a **week-over-week** style rhythm, depending on how often your team runs it). You can **open the shop** from the screen to see the real site anytime.

**Optional summary:** Sometimes you can ask for a **short written summary** built from those snapshot numbers (for example, which shop grew or shrank). That text is **only an aid** based on what was captured—it is **not** a substitute for visiting the shop or reading their offers yourself.

**Purpose:** Spot **early shifts** in how much inventory competitors surface online, without manually counting listings every day.

## Sourcing directory — what the links are, and how to update them

**What you are looking at**

- The page is a **procurement map**: **regions** (for example Germany, Netherlands, France, Italy, Austria, plus an accessories section) and, under each, a **table of platforms**.
- Each row is a **real shop or marketplace link** (opens in a new tab), with **type** (for example certified refurbisher, private classifieds, dealer outlet), an **indicative EUR price range** from research (not a live quote), a short **B2B access note** where relevant, and a **confidence** flag (how solid the research behind that row is).
- **Important:** This directory does **not** feed the main listing grid by itself. The home list comes from **imports your team configures**. The directory is there so **you** can **plan** where to look next, negotiate B2B access, or compare regions—**next to** the live data, not instead of it.

**If you want links or notes changed**

- When you find a **better platform**, a **broken link**, or a **correction** (wrong type, outdated B2B note, etc.), send **Jan** the details in ordinary language—for example: **name of the platform**, **country**, **full web address**, **what kind of seller it is** (classifieds vs refurb vs dealer), and **any B2B comment** you want shown.
- Jan updates the **fixed list inside the product** so the sourcing table matches your latest procurement picture. After your team **publishes an updated version** of the app, everyone sees the new links and text on that page.

You do **not** need to know how that update is done technically—think of it as **editing the master copy** of the directory when you send changes.

## Websites the system uses for data

These are the **public sites** the product uses for **pulling offers**, **snapshots**, or **reference**. Each site’s own rules are respected. What is active for you depends on how your team sets up imports and timing.

**Swiss-focused**

- **veloplus.ch** — Veloplus shop (e-bike category pages the tool reads from).
- **upway.ch** — Upway Switzerland storefront.
- **rebike.ch** — Rebike Switzerland storefront.
- **bibibike.ch** — BibiBike (e.g. bike listing pages).
- **velocorner.ch** — Velocorner marketplace.

**Germany / EU storefronts (examples)**

- **rebike.de** — Rebike Germany (often used for offers).
- **upway.de** — Upway Germany.
- **upway.nl** — Upway Netherlands.
- **upway.co** (France) — Upway FR storefront.
- **likedbikes.com** — LikedBikes.

**Also named in the competitor-watch copy (what Hamza monitors)**

- **Veloplus** — occasions / shop front (as in the UI text).
- **Upway** — Swiss inventory angle (**upway.ch**).
- **Rebike** — Swiss angle (**rebike.ch**).
- **BibiBike** (**bibibike.ch**).
- **Velocorner** (**velocorner.ch**).

**Euro prices in francs**

- Public **euro-to-Swiss-franc** rates are used so prices from Germany and other euro countries can be shown in **CHF** for comparison (this is not a bike shop—just the standard rate everyone can look up).

**Sourcing directory**

- The **Sourcing** page adds **many more named platforms** per country (classifieds, refurb networks, dealer outlets, some wholesale-style notes) in a **read-only reference table**. Those rows are **maintained from your sourcing research** and updated when you send changes through Jan—not the same pipeline as hourly offer imports.
